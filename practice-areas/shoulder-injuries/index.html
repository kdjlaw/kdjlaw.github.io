Shoulder Injuries

New Jersey Shoulder Injuries Attorneys

Shoulder injuries prevent injury victims from working and doing the things they do on a normal everyday basis. At the law office of Kessler, Digiovanni & Jesuele, our lawyers recover financial damages associated with accidents caused by the negligence of others. In many cases, shoulder injuries involved other serious medical complications such as nerve and muscle damage, as well as chronic pain. When complications arise, dexterity and strength may diminish, leading to short and long-term disability. In order to ensure our clients recover financial damages proportional to the severity of their shoulder injury, our attorneys work closely with medical experts and economists in determining the financial impact of a shoulder injury.

If a shoulder injury has left you facing lost wages and medical bills, contact the personal injury lawyers at Kessler, Digiovanni & Jesuele today and schedule a free consultation to discuss your case.

Determining Your Losses - What Insurance Companies Won't Tell You

Most insurance companies use a software program called "Colossus" to determine claims. What a claims agent puts into the computer, as well as the values and information included in the program initially, determines the amount proposed. In the vast majority of cases, Colossus does not take into consideration costs associated with pain and suffering or loss in quality of life. Additionally, claims settlements are determined in large part by computer algorithms and not by human judgment and discernment. Industry insiders have also claimed cost values associated with certain kinds of injuries are purposely reduced by changing certain benchmark values.

How We Protect Our Clients

As personal injury attorneys, we understand how insurance companies operate and the tactics they use to reduce claim amounts and payouts. Our attorneys work closely with medical experts and economists who can establish the scope and financial impact of shoulder injuries. Working with established cost data from HMOs and insurance companies themselves, we're prepared to expose inadequate estimates on the part of claims adjusters.
