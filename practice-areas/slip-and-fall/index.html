New Jersey Personal Injury Lawyers Helping People Recover Damages for Injuries Suffered as the Result of Slip & Falls / Unsafe Property

Landlords, storeowners, and residential property owners sometimes allow unsafe conditions to exist on their land. Dangerous property conditions can cause serious injury to an unsuspecting victim. Here are a few examples.

  • A wet floor in a supermarket in East Orange, New Jersey, causes a patron to slip and fall rupturing a disc in her back. No warning was given of the wet floor, despite the fact that management was aware of moisture leaking from a refrigerator unit for more than two hours.
  • An apartment building owner in Plainfield, NJ, fails to provide adequate building security, and a tenant is assaulted and robbed in a hallway, suffering head injuries and a broken arm.
  • A residential owner in Bergen County, New Jersey, fails to enclose his swimming pool. A toddling child from the neighborhood falls into the pool and drowns.

Dangerous property conditions, which the owner or property manager could have foreseen causing the accident that occurred, can create legal liability for the owner.
