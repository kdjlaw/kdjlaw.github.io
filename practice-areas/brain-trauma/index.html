Brain Trauma

Westfield, New Jersey, Brain Trauma Lawyers

Brain trauma often leads to serious long-term medical conditions that can impact a person's ability to work and lead a normal, independent life. At the Westfield, New Jersey law office of Kessler, Digiovanni & Jesuele, our personal injury attorneys work closely with neurologists, psychologists, and other medical experts in determining the scope of our client's injuries and the future medical treatment they will need as a result. We consult medical economists and life care planners in order to quantify how much future surgeries, physical therapy, special education, medical equipment, and lost wages will cost, in addition to diminished quality of life. We humanize our client, taking steps to ensure jurors understand how brain trauma has effected you and your family.

To schedule a free consultation and discuss how we can help you recover financial damages proportional to your losses, contact the brain trauma attorneys at Kessler, Digiovanni & Jesuele today.

Short and Long-Term Consequences of Brain Trauma

Brain injuries can result in both immediate and long-term physical consequences. At the law office of Kessler, Digiovanni & Jesuele, our personal injury lawyers represent victims of head injuries involving the following:

  • Memory loss
  • Brain damage
  • Cognitive impairment
  • Chronic pain
  • Loss of balance
  • Loss of dexterity
  • Tingling or loss of sensation in the extremities
  • Severe headaches
  • Spinal cord injury

Holding Negligent People Responsible - Accidents and Brain Trauma

While head injuries can occur in any number of ways, our office has the investigative resources to expose negligence on the part of employers, manufacturers, doctors, property owners and auto drivers involving the following:

  • Car accidents
  • Truck accidents
  • Motorcycle accidents
  • Pedestrian knockdowns
  • Bicycle accidents
  • Construction site accidents
  • Falling tiles, debris, or merchandise
  • Boating or water related accidents
  • Slippery floors, crumbling stairs
  • Malfunctioning escalators

Protect Yourself before an Insurance Claims Adjuster Calls

Insurance companies realize it may take weeks or months for the full impact of a closed head injury to become apparent. As a result, they have a financial incentive to settle your case as quickly as possible before other medical needs associated with your injury become evident. At Kessler, Digiovanni & Jesuele, our attorneys protect your rights, handling all forms of communication between you and an insurer. We make sure you aren't misled into settling a claim quickly or signing a statement that could hurt your later.
