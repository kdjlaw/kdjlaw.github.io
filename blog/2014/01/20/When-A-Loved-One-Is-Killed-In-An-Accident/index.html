When A Loved One Is Killed In An Accident

Posted on 01/20/14 by Vincent Jesuele

The unexpected death of a family member as a result of an accident can present one with most difficult times. A family is left not only to mourn the loss of a spouse, parent, or child, but to bear the financial burden of the funeral costs and burial. If a loved one was killed in an automobile accident, a fall accident, or while on the job, you and your family may be eligible for compensation. Here is what you need to know:

Auto Accident

When death occurs as a result of an auto accident, there are two sources of benefits. The automobile insurance policy of the deceased or a resident family member provides a death and funeral benefit as well as payment for all accident related medical bills. In addition, the party at fault for the accident may be sued for all damages flowing from the accident including pain and suffering, unpaid medical bills, and past and future wage loss.

Fall Accident

When death occurs due to fall down or complications due to the fall down, the insurance policy of the premises or property where the fall occurred may provide medical and funeral benefits. In addition, the owner of the property or premises can be sued for damages flowing from the fall if the fall occurred due to a defect or dangerous condition of the premises.
